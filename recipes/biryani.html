The recipe of Biryani

WHAT IS BIRYANI, AND WHY DO I LOVE IT SO MUCH?

Essentially, it’s your favourite chicken curry (or vegetable or other protein of choice) buried under a mound of delicately spiced fluffy rice, all
made in one pot. The rice is steamed over a low heat so it absorbs the flavours of the curry bubbling away underneath.

So in a nutshell, it’s every curry loving-carb monsters’ dream come true. It’s got my name written all over it!

Ingredients Required

  • Chicken (1.1 lbs)
  • Basmati rice (2 cups)
  • Yogurt (3 tablespoons)
  • Ginger-garlic paste (1¼ tablespoons)
  • Lemon juice
  • Green chili (optional)
  • Vegetable oil
  • Spices: biryani masala, turmeric powder, Kashmiri red chili powder, coriander powder, roasted cumin powder, salt
  • Fresh herbs: mint leaves, coriander leaves
  • Onion (sliced)
  • Saffron strands (pinch)
  • Milk (2 tablespoons)

Instructions

  1. Marinate the chicken: Wash and cut the chicken into pieces. In a large bowl, combine yogurt, lemon juice, ginger-garlic paste, green chili,
    oil, spices (biryani masala, turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, salt), and chopped mint and coriander leaves.
    Mix well, coating the chicken evenly. Cover and marinate for at least 30 minutes, or preferably overnight in the refrigerator for deeper flavor.
  2. Prepare the rice: Rinse the basmati rice in water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in clean water for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  3. Cook the onions:Heat oil in a large pot or Dutch oven. Sauté the sliced onions until golden brown. Remove the onions with a slotted spoon
    and set them aside.
  4. Cook the marinated chicken:In the same pot with the remaining oil, add the marinated chicken. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally,
    until the chicken is browned on all sides.
  5. Layer the biryani:Add a layer of cooked chicken to the bottom of the pot. Sprinkle with some fried onions and a few strands of saffron
    (soaked in milk). Now, add half of the drained rice. Season with salt and more saffron milk. Repeat the layering process with the remaining
    chicken, fried onions, rice, and saffron milk.
  6. Seal the pot:Here's a traditional touch: make a dough using flour and water. Seal the rim of the pot with this dough to trap the steam and
    aromas. Alternatively, you can use aluminum foil to cover the pot tightly.
  7. Cook the biryani:Bring the pot to a boil over high heat. Then, reduce heat to low and simmer for 30-35 minutes, or until the rice is cooked
    through.
  8. Rest and serve:Turn off the heat and let the biryani rest for 10-15 minutes with the lid on. This allows the flavors to develop further. Fluff
    the rice gently with a fork before serving. Enjoy your delicious homemade chicken biryani!
